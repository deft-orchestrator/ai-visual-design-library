# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

* **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* **Giving and gracefully accepting** constructive feedback
* **Accepting responsibility** and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience
* **Focusing on what is best** not just for us as individuals, but for the overall
  community
* **Showing empathy** towards other community members
* **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
* **Being patient** with newcomers and helping them learn
* **Celebrating** others' successes and contributions

### Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of
  any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address,
  without their explicit permission
* Spam, advertising, or solicitation
* Impersonation of other community members or project maintainers
* Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following (online or in person)
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting
* Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior

---

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including but not limited to:

* GitHub repositories (code, issues, pull requests, discussions)
* Discord server (all channels)
* Twitter and other social media when representing the project
* Email communications
* Community events (online and offline)
* Any other forums or platforms where community members interact

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing
the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include:
* Using an official project e-mail address
* Posting via an official social media account
* Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

üìß **conduct@aivdl.dev**

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

When reporting, please include:
* Your contact information
* Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved
* Description of the incident
* Date and time of the incident
* Context or background information
* Any supporting evidence (screenshots, links, etc.)
* If you believe this incident is ongoing

### Confidentiality

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident. We will keep your identity confidential unless:
* You give us explicit permission to share your identity
* We are legally required to disclose your identity
* There is a risk to someone's safety

### Response Timeline

* **Within 24 hours**: Acknowledgment of report
* **Within 7 days**: Investigation and decision
* **Within 14 days**: Appeal review (if applicable)

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining
the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing
clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the
behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of
actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No
interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This
includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels
like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent
ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or
private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction
with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period.
Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the
community.

## Appeals Process

If you believe you have been falsely or unfairly accused of violating this Code
of Conduct, you may appeal the decision by:

1. **Sending an email** to conduct@aivdl.dev within 7 days of the decision
2. **Including** a concise description of your appeal and any supporting evidence
3. **Awaiting** a response from the appeals committee within 14 days

The appeals committee will consist of community leaders who were not involved in
the original decision.

## Additional Guidelines

### For Contributors

* **Be patient** with code reviews and feedback
* **Assume good intentions** - most people are trying their best
* **Ask for help** when you need it - we're all learning
* **Celebrate others** - recognize contributions large and small
* **Share knowledge** - help newcomers get started

### For Maintainers

* **Respond promptly** to issues and pull requests
* **Be constructive** in code reviews
* **Explain decisions** - transparency builds trust
* **Recognize contributions** - thank people for their work
* **Set boundaries** - it's okay to say no with kindness

### For Community Members

* **Welcome newcomers** - remember when you were new
* **Share resources** - help others learn and grow
* **Stay on topic** - keep discussions relevant
* **Be kind in disagreements** - focus on ideas, not people
* **Report problems** - help keep the community safe

---

## Specific Platform Guidelines

### GitHub

* Keep issues focused on technical problems or feature requests
* Use discussions for open-ended questions
* Be respectful in code reviews
* Don't spam with "+1" comments - use reactions instead

### Discord

* Follow channel topics
* Use threads for extended discussions
* Don't @mention maintainers unless urgent
* Keep memes and off-topic chat in appropriate channels
* Be mindful of different time zones

### Social Media

* Don't speak for the project unless authorized
* Be respectful when sharing project content
* Credit others when sharing their work
* Assume public visibility - screenshots travel

## Diversity and Inclusion Statement

AIVDL is committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all
contributors and community members. We actively welcome people of all:

* Backgrounds and identities
* Experience levels (from beginners to experts)
* Countries and time zones
* Languages (we support non-English speakers)
* Abilities and disabilities

We believe diversity strengthens our community and improves our project. We are
committed to:

* **Accessible communication** - clear language, multiple formats
* **Inclusive decision-making** - diverse voices in governance
* **Equitable opportunities** - support for underrepresented groups
* **Continuous improvement** - learning and adapting our practices
